# B.Sc. Engineering 2nd Year Odd Semester Examination 2019
**Course No: Math 2117**
**Course Title: Vector Analysis & Linear Algebra**
**Time: 3 Hours**
**Full Marks: 72**

---

### SECTION - A

**1(a)** Find an equation for the plane perpendicular to the vector $\bar{A} = 2\hat{i} + 3\hat{j} + 6\hat{k}$ and passing through the terminal point of the vector $\bar{B} = \hat{i} + 5\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}$. **(04)**

**1(b)** Find the volume of the parallelepiped whose edges are represented by the vector $\bar{A} = 2\hat{i} - 3\hat{j} + 4\hat{k}$, $\bar{B} = \hat{i} + 2\hat{j} - \hat{k}$ and $\bar{C} = 3\hat{i} - \hat{j} + 2\hat{k}$. **(04)**

**1(c)** Find the acute angles which the line joining the points $(1, -3, 2)$ and $(3, -5, 1)$ makes with the coordinate axes. **(04)**

**2(a)** A particle moves along the curve $x = 2t^2, y = t^2 - 4t, z = 3t - 5$, where $t$ is the time. Find the components of its velocity and acceleration at time $t=1$ in the direction $\hat{i} - 3\hat{j} + 2\hat{k}$. **(04)**

**2(b)** If $\bar{A}$ has a constant magnitude show that $\bar{A}$ and $\frac{d\bar{A}}{dt}$ are perpendicular provided $\left|\frac{d\bar{A}}{dt}\right| \neq 0$. **(04)**

**2(c)** Find the directional derivative of $\phi = x^2yz + 4xz^2$ at $(1, -2, 1)$ in the direction $2\hat{i} - \hat{j} - 2\hat{k}$. **(04)**

**3(a)** Find the angle between the surfaces $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 9$ and $z = x^2 + y^2 - 3$ at the point $(2, -1, 2)$. **(04)**

**3(b)** Prove that $\nabla^2(1/r) = 0$. **(04)**

**3(c)** If $\bar{F}$ represents a conservative force field then show that $\nabla \times \bar{F} = 0$. **(04)**

**4(a)** Evaluate $\iiint_V \nabla \cdot \bar{F} dV$ where $\bar{F} = (2x^2 - 3z)\hat{i} - 2xy\hat{j} - 4x\hat{k}$ and $V$ is the closed region bounded by the planes $x = 0, y = 0, z = 0$ and $2x + 2y + z = 4$. **(06)**

**4(b)** Verify Green's theorem in the plane for $\oint_C (2x - y^2)dx - xydy$ where $C$ is the boundary of the region enclosed by the circles $x^2 + y^2 = 1$ and $x^2 + y^2 = 9$. **(06)**

### SECTION - B

**5(a)** What is an inverse of a matrix? Find the inverse of the following matrix: **(06)**

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 3 & 3 \\
1 & 4 & 3 \\
1 & 3 & 4
\end{pmatrix}
$$

**5(b)** What is the normal form of a matrix? Find the rank of the following matrix reducing it to the normal form: **(06)**

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
3 & -2 & 0 & -7 \\
0 & 2 & 1 & -5 \\
1 & -2 & -2 & 1 \\
0 & 1 & 1 & -6
\end{pmatrix}
$$

**6(a)** Discuss about the consistency of a system and then find the solution set of the system: **(06)**

$$
\begin{aligned}
x + 2y + 2z &= 1 \\
2x + y + z &= 2 \\
3x + 2y + 2z &= 3 \\
y + z &= 0
\end{aligned}
$$

**6(b)** Find the trivial solution of: **(06)**

$$
\begin{aligned}
x + y + z + w &= 0 \\
x + 3y - 2z + 4w &= 0 \\
2x + y - z - w &= 0
\end{aligned}
$$

> [!NOTE]
> **Audit & Errata Details:**
> The third equation was cut off by the phone watermark in the original image. Analysis of the student's handwritten solutions at the bottom of the page confirmed the third equation is $2x + y - z - w = 0$, leading to the non-trivial solution $(x,y,z,w) = (-4k, 6k, k, -3k)$ for parameter $k$. Although the print asks for the "trivial solution" (which is always $(0,0,0,0)$), the question mathematically intended to find the non-trivial solution.

**7(a)** Find the eigen vectors of the following matrix: **(06)**

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 3 & 0 \\
3 & -2 & -1 \\
0 & -1 & 1
\end{pmatrix}
$$

**7(b)** Verify Cayley-Hamilton's theorem for $A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 & -1 \\ 1 & -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$ and hence find $A^{-1}$. **(06)**

**8(a)** Prove that the vector space $V$ is the direct sum of its subspaces $U$ and $W$ if and only if (i) $V = U+W$, (ii) $U \cap W = \{0\}$. **(06)**

**8(b)** Define Kernel and image of a linear mapping. Let $F : V \rightarrow U$ be a linear mapping. Show that the Kernel of $F$ is a subspace of $V$ and the image of $F$ is a subspace of $U$. **(06)**
