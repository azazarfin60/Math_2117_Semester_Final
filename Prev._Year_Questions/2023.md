# B.Sc. Engineering 2nd Year Odd Semester Examination, 2023
**Course No: Math 2117**
**Course Title: Vector Analysis & Linear Algebra**
**Time: 03 Hours**
**Full Marks: 60**

---

### SECTION - A

**1(a)** Determine a unit vector perpendicular to the plane of

$$
\bar{A} = 2\hat{i} - 6\hat{j} - 3\hat{k}
$$

and

$$
\bar{B} = 4\hat{i} + 3\hat{j} - \hat{k}
$$

. **(03)**

**1(b)** Find the area of a triangle with vertices at $(3, -1, 2)$, $(1, -1, -3)$ and $(4, -3, 1)$. **(03)**

**1(c)** Given the space curve $x = t, y = t^2, z = \frac{2}{3}t^3$, find the torsion $\tau$. **(04)**

**2(a)** Determine the angles $\alpha, \beta$, and $\gamma$ which the vector

$$
\vec{r} = x\hat{i} + y\hat{j} + z\hat{k}
$$

makes with the positive directions of the coordinate axes and show that $\cos^2\alpha + \cos^2\beta + \cos^2\gamma = 1$. **(03)**

**2(b)** Find the constant 'a' such that the vectors

$$
2\hat{i} - \hat{j} + \hat{k}
$$

$$
\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} - 3\hat{k}
$$

and

$$
3\hat{i} + a\hat{j} + 5\hat{k}
$$

are coplanar. **(03)**

**2(c)** A particle moves along the curve $x = 2t^2, y = t^2 - 4t, z = 3t - 5$, where $t$ is the time. Find the components of its velocity and acceleration at time $t=1$ in the direction

$$
\hat{i} - 3\hat{j} + 2\hat{k}
$$

. **(04)**

**3(a)** State and Prove Stokes' theorem. **(05)**

**3(b)** Verify Green's theorem in the plane for

$$
\oint_C (xy + y^2)dx + x^2dy
$$

Where $C$ is the closest curve of the region bounded by $y = x$ and $y = x^2$. **(05)**

> [!NOTE]
> **Errata in Original Paper:**
> The original paper printed "closest curve" instead of "closed curve".

**4(a)** Define commutative, idempotent and involutory matrix. **(02)**

**4(b)** Prove that $(AB)^{-1} = B^{-1}A^{-1}$. **(02)**

**4(c)** Reduce the following matrix to the canonical form: **(03)**

$$
A = \begin{bmatrix}
0 & 0 & 1 & 3 & -2 \\
0 & 1 & 2 & 6 & 0 \\
0 & 2 & 3 & 9 & 2 \\
0 & 1 & 1 & 3 & 2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

**4(d)** Find the rank of the following matrix: **(03)**

$$
A = \begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 & 2 \\
2 & 3 & 5 & 1 \\
1 & 3 & 4 & 5
\end{bmatrix}
$$

### SECTION - B

**5(a)** Show that every square matrix can be expressed as the sum of a Hermitian matrix and a Skew-Hermitian matrix. **(05)**

**5(b)** Find the inverse of the matrix

$$
A = \begin{bmatrix}
1 & 3 & 5 \\
2 & 4 & 5 \\
3 & 7 & 6
\end{bmatrix}
$$

using row transformation. **(05)**

**6(a)** Find the eigen values and eigen vectors of the matrix: **(05)**

$$
A = \begin{bmatrix}
2 & 1 & 1 \\
-1 & 2 & -1 \\
1 & -1 & 2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

**6(b)** Solve the following system of linear equations: **(05)**

$$
\begin{aligned}
x + 2y - z &= 2 \\
2x + y + z &= 1 \\
x + 5y - 4z &= 5
\end{aligned}
$$

**7(a)** Consider the vectors $v\_1 = (2, 1, 4), v\_2 = (1, -1, 3)$ and $v\_3 = (3, 2, 5)$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$. Show that $v = (5, 9, 5)$ is a linear combination of $v\_1, v\_2$, and $v\_3$. **(05)**

**7(b)** Let $U$ be the subspace of $\mathbb{R}^3$ spanned by the vectors $(1, 2, 1), (0, -1, 0)$ and $(2, 0, 2)$. Find a basis and the dimension of $U$. **(05)**

> [!NOTE]
> **Errata in Original Paper:**
> The exam paper reads "Let U be the surface of $\mathbb{R}^3$ spanned by...", which is a misprint for "subspace".

**8(a)** Define sum and direct sum with examples. **(02)**

**8(b)** Let $w$ be the space generated by the polynomials: **(04)**

$$
\begin{aligned}
v_1 &= t^3 - 2t^2 + 4t + 1 \\
v_2 &= 2t^3 - 3t^2 + 9t - 1 \\
v_3 &= t^3 + 6t - 5 \\
v_4 &= 2t^3 - 5t^2 + 7t + 5
\end{aligned}
$$

Find a basis and the dimension of $w$.

**8(c)** Identify that the following mappings $F$ are linear or not linear: **(04)**
*   (i) $F : \mathbb{R}^3 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$ defined by $F(x, y, z) = 2x - 3y + 4z$
*   (ii) $F : \mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3$ defined by $F(x, y) = (x+1, 2y, x+y)$

> [!NOTE]
> **Errata in Original Paper:**
> The domain of the second mapping was printed as $\mathbb{R}^v$, which is a typo for $\mathbb{R}^2$.
