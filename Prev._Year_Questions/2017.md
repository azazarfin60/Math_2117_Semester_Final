### SECTION - A

**1(a)** Find the angle between the surfaces $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 9$ and $z = x^2 + y^2 - 3$ at $(2, -1, 2)$. **(04)**

**1(b)** If

$$
\vec{F} = 3xy\hat{i} - y^2\hat{j}
$$

, then evaluate

$$
\int_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}
$$

, when $C$ is the curve in the $xy$ plane, $y = 2x^2$ from $(0,0)$ to $(1,2)$. **(04)**

**1(c)** Find the directional derivative of $\phi = 4e^{2x - y + z}$ at $(1, 1, -1)$ in a direction toward the point $(-3, 5, 6)$. **(04)**

**2(a)** If

$$
\vec{F} = (2x + y^2)\hat{i} + (3y - 4x)\hat{j}
$$

, then evaluate

$$
\oint_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}
$$

around the curve $C$ of the following figure: **(06)**

> [!NOTE]
> **Diagram Description — Green's Theorem Region**
> The curve $C$ is a closed boundary in the $xy$-plane consisting of two parabolic segments starting from the origin $(0,0)$ and meeting at the point $(1,1)$:
> 1. A lower path along the parabola $y = x^2$ directed from $(0,0)$ to $(1,1)$ (indicated by a rightward arrow).
> 2. An upper path along the parabola $y^2 = x$ (or $y = \sqrt{x}$) directed from $(1,1)$ to $(0,0)$ (indicated by a leftward arrow).
> The closed path is traversed in the counterclockwise direction.

**2(b)** Evaluate

$$
\iint_S \vec{A} \cdot \hat{n} dS

, where

$$
\vec{A} = z\hat{i} + x\hat{j} - 3y^2z\hat{k}
$$

and $S$ is the surface of the cylinder $x^2 + y^2 = 16$ included in the first octant between $z=0$ and $z=5$. **(06)**

**3(a)** State and prove Green's theorem in the plane. **(06)**

### SECTION - B

**5(a)** If

$$
A_\alpha = \begin{pmatrix}
\cos\alpha & \sin\alpha \\
-\sin\alpha & \cos\alpha
\end{pmatrix}
$$

, then show that $A\_\alpha \cdot A\_\beta = A\_{\alpha+\beta} = A\_\beta \cdot A\_\alpha$. **(03)**

**5(b)** Find the adjoint and inverse of

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
0 & 1 & 2 \\
1 & 2 & 3 \\
3 & 1 & 1
\end{pmatrix}
$$

. **(05)**

**5(c)** Determine the characteristic roots of

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 \\
0 & -4 & 2 \\
0 & 0 & 7
\end{pmatrix}
$$

. **(04)**

**6(a)** What is the rank of a matrix? Find it for

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
0 & 0 & 1 & -3 & -2 \\
0 & 1 & 2 & 6 & 0 \\
0 & 2 & 3 & 9 & 2 \\
0 & 1 & 1 & 3 & 2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

. **(06)**

**6(b)** Solve by elementary transformation: **(06)**

$$
\begin{aligned}
2x_1 + 2x_2 + x_3 &= 2 \\
3x_1 + x_2 - 2x_3 &= 1 \\
4x_1 - 3x_2 - x_3 &= 3
\end{aligned}
$$

**7** What are the eigen-values and eigen-vectors? Find them for the matrix

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
2 & 2 & 1 \\
1 & 3 & 1 \\
1 & 2 & 2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

. **(12)**

**8** Diagonalize the matrix

$$
A = \begin{bmatrix}
1 & 1 \\
0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

. Hence evaluate $e^A$, and then solve $\frac{dx\_1}{dt} = x\_2$, $\frac{dx\_2}{dt} = x\_1$. **(12)**

> [!WARNING]
> **Errata in Original Paper:**
> The matrix

$$
A = \begin{bmatrix}
1 & 1 \\
0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

is not diagonalizable because it has a repeated eigenvalue $\lambda = 1$ with a geometric multiplicity of 1.
