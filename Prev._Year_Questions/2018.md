# B.Sc. Engineering 2nd Year Odd Semester Examination 2018
**Course No: Math 2117**
**Course Title: Vector Analysis & Linear Algebra**
**Time: 3 Hours**
**Full Marks: 72**

---

### SECTION - A

**1(a)** Show that the vectors

$$
\bar{A} = 2\hat{i} + \hat{j} - 3\hat{k}, \quad \bar{B} = \hat{i} - 4\hat{k}, \quad \bar{C} = 4\hat{i} + 3\hat{j} - \hat{k}
$$

are linearly dependent and determine a relation between them and hence show that the terminal points are collinear. **(06)**

**1(b)** Find the constants $a, b, c$ so that

$$
\bar{V} = (x + 2y + az)\hat{i} + (bx - 3y - z)\hat{j} + (4x + cy + 2z)\hat{k}
$$

is irrotational. **(03)**

**1(c)** If $\bar{A}$ is a constant vector, then prove that $\nabla(\bar{r} \cdot \bar{A}) = \bar{A}$. **(03)**

**2(a)** Prove that

$$
\bar{F} = (y^2 \cos x + z^3)\hat{i} + (2y \sin x - 4)\hat{j} + (3xz^2 + 2)\hat{k}
$$

represents a conservative force field. Also find the scalar potential for $\bar{F}$. **(06)**

**2(b)** If

$$
\bar{A} = (3x^2 + 6y)\hat{i} - 14yz\hat{j} + 20xz^2\hat{k}
$$

, then evaluate

$$
\int_C \bar{A} \cdot d\bar{r}
$$

from $(0,0,0)$ to $(1,1,1)$ along the following paths $C$: **(06)**
*   (i) $x = t, y = t^2, z = t^3$
*   (ii) the straight line joining $(0,0,0)$ to $(1,1,1)$.

**3(a)** Let $\Phi = 45x^2y$ and let $V$ denote the closed region bounded by the planes $4x + 2y + z = 8$, $x = 0, y = 0, z = 0$. Evaluate

$$
\iiint_V \Phi dV
$$

. **(06)**

**3(b)** Verify the divergence theorem for

$$
\bar{A} = 4x\hat{i} - 2y^2\hat{j} + z^2\hat{k}
$$

taken over the region bounded by

$$
x^2 + y^2 = 4, \quad z = 0, \quad z = 3
$$

. **(06)**

**4(a)** Show that $\nabla \times (\nabla \times \bar{A}) = -\nabla^2\bar{A} + \nabla(\nabla \cdot \bar{A})$. **(05)**

**4(b)** Find an equation for the tangent plane to the surface $2xz^2 - 3xy - 4x = 7$ at the point $(1, -1, 2)$. **(03)**

**4(c)** What is directional derivative? Find the directional derivative of $\phi = x^2yz + 4xz^2$ at $(1, -2, -1)$ in the direction

$$
2\hat{i} - \hat{j} - 2\hat{k}
$$

. **(04)**

### SECTION - B

**5(a)** Define symmetric, skew-symmetric, and Hermitian matrices with examples. **(03)**

**5(b)** Define singular matrix. If $A$ and $B$ are non-singular matrices of the same order, then show that $(AB)^{-1} = B^{-1}A^{-1}$. Hence prove that $(A^{-1})^n = (A^n)^{-1}$ for any positive integer. **(05)**

**5(c)** What is rank of a matrix? Find the rank of

$$
A = \begin{pmatrix}
1 & -2 & 1 & -1 \\
1 & 1 & -2 & 3 \\
4 & 1 & -5 & 8 \\
5 & -7 & 2 & -1
\end{pmatrix}
$$

. **(04)**

**6(a)** Find the value of $\lambda$ so that the following equations have a solution and solve completely in each case: **(06)**

$$
\begin{aligned}
x + y + z &= 1 \\
x + 2y + 4z &= \lambda \\
x + 4y + 10z &= \lambda^2
\end{aligned}
$$

**6(b)** Solve: **(06)**

$$
\begin{aligned}
2x_1 - x_2 + x_3 &= 0 \\
3x_1 + 2x_2 + x_3 &= 0 \\
x_1 - 3x_2 + 5x_3 &= 0
\end{aligned}
$$

**7(a)** Define eigen values and eigen vectors. Find the eigen values and associated eigen vectors of

$$
B = \begin{pmatrix}
2 & 2 & 1 \\
1 & 3 & 1 \\
1 & 2 & 2
\end{pmatrix}
$$

. **(06)**

**7(b)** State and prove Cayley-Hamilton theorem. **(06)** *(Note: Spelled "Calley-Hamilton" in the original question paper).*

**8(a)** Define vector space and subspace with examples. **(03)**

**8(b)** Define linearly dependent and independent vectors. Determine whether or not the vectors in $\mathbb{R}^3$ are linearly dependent: $(1, 2, -3)$, $(1, -3, 2)$, $(2, -1, 5)$. **(04)**

**8(c)** Define basis and dimension. Let $w$ be the subspace of $\mathbb{R}^4$ generated by the vectors $(1, -2, 5, -3)$, $(2, 3, 1, -4)$ and $(3, 8, -3, -5)$. Find a basis and the dimension of $w$. **(05)**
