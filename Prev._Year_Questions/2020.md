# B.Sc. Engineering 2nd Year Odd Semester Examination 2020
**Course No: MATH 2117**
**Course Title: Vector Analysis & Linear Algebra**
**Time: 3 Hours**
**Full Marks: 72**

---

### SECTION - A

**1(a)** What is the vector? Find a unit vector parallel to the resultant of vectors $\vec{r}\_1 = 2\hat{i} + 4\hat{j} - 5\hat{k}$, $\vec{r}\_2 = \hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + \hat{k}$. **(04)**

**1(b)** Define triple product of vectors. Show that, $\bar{A} \cdot (\bar{B} \times \bar{C})$ is in absolute value equal to the volume of a parallelepiped with sides $\bar{A}$, $\bar{B}$ and $\bar{C}$. **(04)**

**1(c)** If $\bar{A} = 2\hat{i} + \hat{j} - 3\hat{k}$ and $\bar{B} = \hat{i} - 2\hat{j} + \hat{k}$, find a vector of magnitude 5 perpendicular to both $\bar{A}$ and $\bar{B}$. **(04)**

**2(a)** A particle moves along a curve whose parametric equations are $x = e^{-t}, y = 2\cos 3t, z = 2\sin 3t$, where $t$ is the time. Find the magnitudes of the velocity and acceleration at $t=0$. **(04)**

**2(b)** For the space curve $x = t, y = t^2, z = \frac{2}{3}t^3$, find the curvature $K$ and the torsion $T$. **(05)**

**2(c)** Show that, $\nabla\Phi$ is a vector perpendicular to the surface $\Phi(x, y, z) = C$, where $C$ is a constant. **(03)**

**3(a)** Show that, $\bar{A} = (6xy + z^3)\hat{i} + (3x^2 - z)\hat{j} + (3xz^2 - y)\hat{k}$ is irrotational. Find $\Phi$ such that $\bar{A} = \nabla\Phi$. **(06)**

**3(b)** Find the work down in moving a particle in the force field $\vec{F} = (3x^2 + 6y)\hat{i} - 14yz\hat{j} + 20xz^2\hat{k}$ along: **(06)**
*   (i) The curve defined by $x = t, y = t^2, z = t^3$ from $t=0$ to $t=1$ *(Note: Typo "form t=0" in the original question).*
*   (ii) The straight line joining $(0, 0, 0)$ and $(1, 1, 1)$.

> [!NOTE]
> **Errata in Original Paper:**
> The original paper printed "work down" instead of "work done", and "form t=0" instead of "from t=0".

**4(a)** Evaluate 

$$
\iint_S \bar{A} \cdot \hat{n} dS
$$

 where, $\bar{A} = z\hat{i} + x\hat{j} - 3y^2z\hat{k}$ and $S$ is the surface of the cylinder $x^2 + y^2 = 16$ included in the first octant between $z=0$ and $z=5$. **(06)**

**4(b)** Verify Green's theorem in the plane for 

$$
\oint_C (xy + y^2)dx + x^2dy
$$

 where $C$ is a closed region bounded by $y = x$ and $y = x^2$. **(06)**

### SECTION - B

**5(a)** For two matrices $A$ and $B$, prove that $(AB)' = B'A'$. **(03)**

**5(b)** Show that every square matrix can be uniquely expressed as the sum of a symmetric and a skew symmetric matrices. **(04)**

**5(c)** Define inverse of a matrix. Find the adjoint of matrix $A$ and hence find $A^{-1}$, where: **(05)**

$$
A = \begin{bmatrix}
\cos\theta & -\sin\theta & 0 \\
\sin\theta & \cos\theta & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

**6(a)** What is the rank of a matrix? Find the rank of the matrix, 

$$
A = \begin{bmatrix}
6 & 1 & 8 & 3 \\
2 & 1 & 0 & 2 \\
4 & -1 & -8 & -3
\end{bmatrix}
$$

. **(05)**

**6(b)** Investigate for what values of $\lambda$ and $\mu$ the following system of equations have (i) no solution, (ii) unique solution, (iii) infinite number of solutions: **(07)**

$$
\begin{aligned}
x + y + z &= 6 \\
x + 2y + 3z &= 10 \\
x + 2y + \lambda z &= \mu
\end{aligned}
$$

> [!NOTE]
> **Errata in Original Paper:**
> The paper text asks for values of "$\gamma$ and $\mu$", but the system uses $\lambda$ and $\mu$. We assume $\gamma$ is a typo for $\lambda$.

**7(a)** Find the eigen values and the corresponding eigen vectors for the matrix 

$$
A = \begin{bmatrix}
1 & 1 & -2 \\
-1 & 2 & 1 \\
0 & 1 & -1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

. **(06)**

**7(b)** Solve the following system of differential equations by matrix method: **(06)**

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{dx}{dt} &= 6x - 3y \\
\frac{dy}{dt} &= 2x + y
\end{aligned}
$$

**8(a)** Write the vector $v = (1, -2, 5)$ as a linear combination of the vectors $e\_1 = (1, 1, 1)$, $e\_2 = (1, 2, 3)$ and $e\_3 = (2, -1, 1)$. **(04)**

**8(b)** Suppose the vectors $u, v, w$ are linearly independent. Show that the vectors $u + v, u - v, u - 2v + w$ are also linearly independent. **(04)**

**8(c)** Let $w$ be the space generated by the polynomials: **(04)**

$$
\begin{aligned}
v_1 &= t^3 - 2t^2 + 4t + 1 \\
v_2 &= 2t^3 - 3t^2 + 9t - 1 \\
v_3 &= t^3 + 6t - 5 \\
v_4 &= 2t^3 - 5t^2 + 7t + 5
\end{aligned}
$$

Find a basis and the dimension of $w$.
