## Type-01: Finding Vectors and Magnitudes Between Two Points

### Solved Problem 25

#### Question

Determine the vector having initial point $P(x_1, y_1, z_1)$ and terminal point $Q(x_2, y_2, z_2)$ and find its magnitude.

#### Solution

Let $O$ be the origin. The position vectors of points $P$ and $Q$ are:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 = x_1\mathbf{i} + y_1\mathbf{j} + z_1\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{r}_2 = x_2\mathbf{i} + y_2\mathbf{j} + z_2\mathbf{k}
$$

By the triangle law of vector addition:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 + \mathbf{PQ} = \mathbf{r}_2
$$

We solve for vector $\mathbf{PQ}$ by subtracting $\mathbf{r}_1$ from both sides:

$$
\mathbf{PQ} = \mathbf{r}_2 - \mathbf{r}_1
$$

$$
\mathbf{PQ} = (x_2\mathbf{i} + y_2\mathbf{j} + z_2\mathbf{k}) - (x_1\mathbf{i} + y_1\mathbf{j} + z_1\mathbf{k})
$$

Group by components:

$$
\mathbf{PQ} = (x_2 - x_1)\mathbf{i} + (y_2 - y_1)\mathbf{j} + (z_2 - z_1)\mathbf{k}
$$

Now calculate the magnitude using the 3D magnitude formula:

$$
|\mathbf{PQ}| = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2 + (z_2 - z_1)^2}
$$

### Supplementary Problem 58

#### Question

The position vectors of points $P$ and $Q$ are:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 = 2\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{r}_2 = 4\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}
$$

Determine $\mathbf{PQ}$ in terms of the unit vectors $\mathbf{i}$, $\mathbf{j}$, $\mathbf{k}$ and find its magnitude.

#### Solution

The vector $\mathbf{PQ}$ starts at point $P$ and ends at point $Q$. It is calculated by subtracting the initial position vector from the terminal position vector:

$$
\mathbf{PQ} = \mathbf{r}_2 - \mathbf{r}_1
$$

Substitute the given vectors:

$$
\mathbf{PQ} = (4\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}) - (2\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k})
$$

Subtract corresponding components:

$$
\mathbf{PQ} = (4 - 2)\mathbf{i} + (-3 - 3)\mathbf{j} + (2 - (-1))\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{PQ} = 2\mathbf{i} - 6\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}
$$

Now calculate the magnitude of vector $\mathbf{PQ}$:

$$
|\mathbf{PQ}| = \sqrt{2^2 + (-6)^2 + 3^2}
$$

$$
|\mathbf{PQ}| = \sqrt{4 + 36 + 9} = \sqrt{49} = 7
$$

## Type-02: Vector Magnitudes, Linear Combinations, and Unit Vectors

### Solved Problem 22

#### Question

Given the following vectors:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 = 3\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{r}_2 = 2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{r}_3 = -\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}
$$

Find the magnitudes of:
(a) $\mathbf{r}_3$,
(b) $\mathbf{r}_1 + \mathbf{r}_2 + \mathbf{r}_3$,
(c) $2\mathbf{r}_1 - 3\mathbf{r}_2 - 5\mathbf{r}_3$.

#### Solution

**Part (a)**

We use the vector magnitude formula for $\mathbf{r}_3$:

$$
|\mathbf{r}_3| = \sqrt{(-1)^2 + 2^2 + 2^2}
$$

$$
|\mathbf{r}_3| = \sqrt{1 + 4 + 4} = \sqrt{9} = 3
$$

**Part (b)**

First, we calculate the sum of the three vectors:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 + \mathbf{r}_2 + \mathbf{r}_3 = (3\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}) + (2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}) + (-\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k})
$$

We group the terms by components:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 + \mathbf{r}_2 + \mathbf{r}_3 = (3+2-1)\mathbf{i} + (-2-4+2)\mathbf{j} + (1-3+2)\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 + \mathbf{r}_2 + \mathbf{r}_3 = 4\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j} + 0\mathbf{k}
$$

Now, we calculate the magnitude:

$$
|\mathbf{r}_1 + \mathbf{r}_2 + \mathbf{r}_3| = \sqrt{4^2 + (-4)^2 + 0^2}
$$

$$
|\mathbf{r}_1 + \mathbf{r}_2 + \mathbf{r}_3| = \sqrt{16 + 16} = \sqrt{32} = 4\sqrt{2}
$$

**Part (c)**

First, we compute the linear combination vector:

$$
\mathbf{A} = 2\mathbf{r}_1 - 3\mathbf{r}_2 - 5\mathbf{r}_3
$$

$$
\mathbf{A} = 2(3\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}) - 3(2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}) - 5(-\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k})
$$

Distribute the scalars:

$$
\mathbf{A} = (6\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}) - (6\mathbf{i} - 12\mathbf{j} - 9\mathbf{k}) - (-5\mathbf{i} + 10\mathbf{j} + 10\mathbf{k})
$$

Combine the components:

$$
\mathbf{A} = (6-6+5)\mathbf{i} + (-4+12-10)\mathbf{j} + (2+9-10)\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{A} = 5\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}
$$

Now, we calculate the magnitude of the resulting vector:

$$
|\mathbf{A}| = \sqrt{5^2 + (-2)^2 + 1^2}
$$

$$
|\mathbf{A}| = \sqrt{25 + 4 + 1} = \sqrt{30}
$$

### Solved Problem 24

#### Question

Find a unit vector parallel to the resultant of vectors:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 = 2\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} - 5\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{r}_2 = \mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}
$$

#### Solution

First, we find the resultant vector $\mathbf{R}$ by adding the two vectors:

$$
\mathbf{R} = \mathbf{r}_1 + \mathbf{r}_2 = (2\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} - 5\mathbf{k}) + (\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k})
$$

$$
\mathbf{R} = (2+1)\mathbf{i} + (4+2)\mathbf{j} + (-5+3)\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{R} = 3\mathbf{i} + 6\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}
$$

Next, we calculate the magnitude of $\mathbf{R}$:

$$
R = |\mathbf{R}| = \sqrt{3^2 + 6^2 + (-2)^2}
$$

$$
R = \sqrt{9 + 36 + 4} = \sqrt{49} = 7
$$

A unit vector parallel to $\mathbf{R}$ has the same direction as $\mathbf{R}$ but a magnitude of 1. We divide the vector by its magnitude:

$$
\mathbf{u} = \frac{\mathbf{R}}{R} = \frac{3\mathbf{i} + 6\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}}{7}
$$

$$
\mathbf{u} = \frac{3}{7}\mathbf{i} + \frac{6}{7}\mathbf{j} - \frac{2}{7}\mathbf{k}
$$

### Supplementary Problem 59

#### Question

If the vectors are:

$$
\mathbf{A} = 3\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} - 4\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{B} = -2\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{C} = \mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}
$$

Find:
(a) $2\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{B} + 3\mathbf{C}$,
(b) $|\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B} + \mathbf{C}|$,
(c) $|3\mathbf{A} - 2\mathbf{B} + 4\mathbf{C}|$,
(d) a unit vector parallel to $3\mathbf{A} - 2\mathbf{B} + 4\mathbf{C}$.

#### Solution

**Part (a)**

We compute the linear combination vector:

$$
\mathbf{V} = 2\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{B} + 3\mathbf{C}
$$

$$
\mathbf{V} = 2(3\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} - 4\mathbf{k}) - (-2\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}) + 3(\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k})
$$

Distribute the scalars:

$$
\mathbf{V} = (6\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} - 8\mathbf{k}) + (2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}) + (3\mathbf{i} + 6\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k})
$$

Combine the components:

$$
\mathbf{V} = (6 + 2 + 3)\mathbf{i} + (-2 - 4 + 6)\mathbf{j} + (-8 + 3 - 3)\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{V} = 11\mathbf{i} + 0\mathbf{j} - 8\mathbf{k} = 11\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{k}
$$

**Part (b)**

First, calculate the sum vector:

$$
\mathbf{S} = \mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B} + \mathbf{C}
$$

$$
\mathbf{S} = (3\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} - 4\mathbf{k}) + (-2\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}) + (\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k})
$$

Group by components:

$$
\mathbf{S} = (3 - 2 + 1)\mathbf{i} + (-1 + 4 + 2)\mathbf{j} + (-4 - 3 - 1)\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{S} = 2\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j} - 8\mathbf{k}
$$

Now calculate the magnitude of the sum vector:

$$
|\mathbf{S}| = \sqrt{2^2 + 5^2 + (-8)^2}
$$

$$
|\mathbf{S}| = \sqrt{4 + 25 + 64} = \sqrt{93}
$$

**Part (c)**

First, calculate the vector:

$$
\mathbf{W} = 3\mathbf{A} - 2\mathbf{B} + 4\mathbf{C}
$$

$$
\mathbf{W} = 3(3\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} - 4\mathbf{k}) - 2(-2\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}) + 4(\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k})
$$

Distribute the scalars:

$$
\mathbf{W} = (9\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} - 12\mathbf{k}) + (4\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j} + 6\mathbf{k}) + (4\mathbf{i} + 8\mathbf{j} - 4\mathbf{k})
$$

Combine the components:

$$
\mathbf{W} = (9 + 4 + 4)\mathbf{i} + (-3 - 8 + 8)\mathbf{j} + (-12 + 6 - 4)\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{W} = 17\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} - 10\mathbf{k}
$$

Now calculate its magnitude:

$$
|\mathbf{W}| = \sqrt{17^2 + (-3)^2 + (-10)^2}
$$

$$
|\mathbf{W}| = \sqrt{289 + 9 + 100} = \sqrt{398}
$$

**Part (d)**

A unit vector parallel to $\mathbf{W}$ is:

$$
\mathbf{u} = \frac{\mathbf{W}}{|\mathbf{W}|}
$$

Using the results from Part (c), we get:

$$
\mathbf{u} = \frac{17\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} - 10\mathbf{k}}{\sqrt{398}}
$$

$$
\mathbf{u} = \frac{17}{\sqrt{398}}\mathbf{i} - \frac{3}{\sqrt{398}}\mathbf{j} - \frac{10}{\sqrt{398}}\mathbf{k}
$$

### Supplementary Problem 60

#### Question

The following forces act on a particle $P$:

$$
\mathbf{F}_1 = 2\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} - 5\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{F}_2 = -5\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{F}_3 = \mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} + 4\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{F}_4 = 4\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}
$$

Find:
(a) the resultant of the forces,
(b) the magnitude of the resultant.

#### Solution

**Part (a)**

The resultant force $\mathbf{R}$ is the vector sum of all individual forces:

$$
\mathbf{R} = \mathbf{F}_1 + \mathbf{F}_2 + \mathbf{F}_3 + \mathbf{F}_4
$$

$$
\mathbf{R} = (2\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} - 5\mathbf{k}) + (-5\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}) + (\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} + 4\mathbf{k}) + (4\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k})
$$

Combine corresponding components:

$$
\mathbf{R} = (2 - 5 + 1 + 4)\mathbf{i} + (3 + 1 - 2 - 3)\mathbf{j} + (-5 + 3 + 4 - 2)\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{R} = 2\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} + 0\mathbf{k} = 2\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j}
$$

**Part (b)**

Calculate the magnitude of the resultant force:

$$
|\mathbf{R}| = \sqrt{2^2 + (-1)^2}
$$

$$
|\mathbf{R}| = \sqrt{4 + 1} = \sqrt{5}
$$

## Type-03: Finding Unknown Scalars in Vector Equations

### Solved Problem 23

#### Question

If the vectors are:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 = 2\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{r}_2 = \mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{r}_3 = -2\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{r}_4 = 3\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k}
$$

Find the scalars $a$, $b$, $c$ such that:

$$
\mathbf{r}_4 = a\mathbf{r}_1 + b\mathbf{r}_2 + c\mathbf{r}_3
$$

#### Solution

We write out the vector equation:

$$
3\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k} = a(2\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}) + b(\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}) + c(-2\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k})
$$

Expand the right side:

$$
3\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k} = (2a\mathbf{i} - a\mathbf{j} + a\mathbf{k}) + (b\mathbf{i} + 3b\mathbf{j} - 2b\mathbf{k}) + (-2c\mathbf{i} + c\mathbf{j} - 3c\mathbf{k})
$$

Group by components:

$$
3\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k} = (2a + b - 2c)\mathbf{i} + (-a + 3b + c)\mathbf{j} + (a - 2b - 3c)\mathbf{k}
$$

Equating the components gives a system of three linear equations:
1) $2a + b - 2c = 3$
2) $-a + 3b + c = 2$
3) $a - 2b - 3c = 5$

We can solve this system. From equation (2), we express $c$ in terms of $a$ and $b$:

$$
c = 2 + a - 3b
$$

Substitute this expression for $c$ into equation (1):

$$
2a + b - 2(2 + a - 3b) = 3
$$

$$
2a + b - 4 - 2a + 6b = 3
$$

$$
7b - 4 = 3 \implies 7b = 7 \implies b = 1
$$

Using $b = 1$, we update our expression for $c$:

$$
c = 2 + a - 3(1) = a - 1
$$

Substitute $b = 1$ and $c = a - 1$ into equation (3):

$$
a - 2(1) - 3(a - 1) = 5
$$

$$
a - 2 - 3a + 3 = 5
$$

$$
-2a + 1 = 5 \implies -2a = 4 \implies a = -2
$$

Now calculate the value of $c$:

$$
c = a - 1 = -2 - 1 = -3
$$

The scalars are:

$$
a = -2, \quad b = 1, \quad c = -3
$$

So the vector equation is:

$$
\mathbf{r}_4 = -2\mathbf{r}_1 + \mathbf{r}_2 - 3\mathbf{r}_3
$$

### Supplementary Problem 44

#### Question

If $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ are non-collinear vectors, find the values of $x$ and $y$ such that $3\mathbf{A} = 2\mathbf{B}$ where:

$$
\mathbf{A} = (x + 4y)\mathbf{a} + (2x + y + 1)\mathbf{b}
$$

$$
\mathbf{B} = (y - 2x + 2)\mathbf{a} + (2x - 3y - 1)\mathbf{b}
$$

#### Solution

We substitute the expressions for $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ into the equation $3\mathbf{A} = 2\mathbf{B}$:

$$
3[(x + 4y)\mathbf{a} + (2x + y + 1)\mathbf{b}] = 2[(y - 2x + 2)\mathbf{a} + (2x - 3y - 1)\mathbf{b}]
$$

Multiply the coefficients by the scalars:

$$
[3(x + 4y)]\mathbf{a} + [3(2x + y + 1)]\mathbf{b} = [2(y - 2x + 2)]\mathbf{a} + [2(2x - 3y - 1)]\mathbf{b}
$$

$$
(3x + 12y)\mathbf{a} + (6x + 3y + 3)\mathbf{b} = (2y - 4x + 4)\mathbf{a} + (4x - 6y - 2)\mathbf{b}
$$

We move all terms to the left side:

$$
[(3x + 12y) - (2y - 4x + 4)]\mathbf{a} + [(6x + 3y + 3) - (4x - 6y - 2)]\mathbf{b} = \mathbf{0}
$$

Simplify the combined coefficients:

$$
(7x + 10y - 4)\mathbf{a} + (2x + 9y + 5)\mathbf{b} = \mathbf{0}
$$

Vectors $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ are non-collinear. A linear combination of non-collinear vectors equals zero if and only if each scalar coefficient is zero.

This gives two linear equations:
1) $7x + 10y - 4 = 0 \implies 7x + 10y = 4$
2) $2x + 9y + 5 = 0 \implies 2x + 9y = -5$

We solve this system. Multiply equation (1) by 2, and equation (2) by 7:

$$
14x + 20y = 8
$$

$$
14x + 63y = -35
$$

Subtract the first equation from the second equation:

$$
(14x + 63y) - (14x + 20y) = -35 - 8
$$

$$
43y = -43 \implies y = -1
$$

Substitute $y = -1$ into equation (2):

$$
2x + 9(-1) = -5
$$

$$
2x - 9 = -5 \implies 2x = 4 \implies x = 2
$$

The required values are $x = 2$ and $y = -1$.

### Supplementary Problem 45

#### Question

The base vectors $\mathbf{a}_1, \mathbf{a}_2, \mathbf{a}_3$ are given in terms of the base vectors $\mathbf{b}_1, \mathbf{b}_2, \mathbf{b}_3$ by the relations:

$$
\mathbf{a}_1 = 2\mathbf{b}_1 + 3\mathbf{b}_2 - \mathbf{b}_3
$$

$$
\mathbf{a}_2 = \mathbf{b}_1 - 2\mathbf{b}_2 + 2\mathbf{b}_3
$$

$$
\mathbf{a}_3 = -2\mathbf{b}_1 + \mathbf{b}_2 - 2\mathbf{b}_3
$$

If $\mathbf{F} = 3\mathbf{b}_1 - \mathbf{b}_2 + 2\mathbf{b}_3$, express $\mathbf{F}$ in terms of $\mathbf{a}_1$, $\mathbf{a}_2$ and $\mathbf{a}_3$.

#### Solution

We want to find three scalars $x, y, z$ such that:

$$
\mathbf{F} = x\mathbf{a}_1 + y\mathbf{a}_2 + z\mathbf{a}_3
$$

Substitute the definitions of $\mathbf{a}_1, \mathbf{a}_2, \mathbf{a}_3$ into the equation:

$$
3\mathbf{b}_1 - \mathbf{b}_2 + 2\mathbf{b}_3 = x(2\mathbf{b}_1 + 3\mathbf{b}_2 - \mathbf{b}_3) + y(\mathbf{b}_1 - 2\mathbf{b}_2 + 2\mathbf{b}_3) + z(-2\mathbf{b}_1 + \mathbf{b}_2 - 2\mathbf{b}_3)
$$

Expand the right side:

$$
3\mathbf{b}_1 - \mathbf{b}_2 + 2\mathbf{b}_3 = (2x\mathbf{b}_1 + 3x\mathbf{b}_2 - x\mathbf{b}_3) + (y\mathbf{b}_1 - 2y\mathbf{b}_2 + 2y\mathbf{b}_3) + (-2z\mathbf{b}_1 + z\mathbf{b}_2 - 2z\mathbf{b}_3)
$$

Group by components:

$$
3\mathbf{b}_1 - \mathbf{b}_2 + 2\mathbf{b}_3 = (2x + y - 2z)\mathbf{b}_1 + (3x - 2y + z)\mathbf{b}_2 + (-x + 2y - 2z)\mathbf{b}_3
$$

Equating the coefficients of $\mathbf{b}_1$, $\mathbf{b}_2$, and $\mathbf{b}_3$ gives a system of linear equations:
1) $2x + y - 2z = 3$
2) $3x - 2y + z = -1$
3) $-x + 2y - 2z = 2$

We solve this system. Subtract equation (3) from equation (1):

$$
(2x + y - 2z) - (-x + 2y - 2z) = 3 - 2
$$

$$
3x - y = 1 \implies y = 3x - 1
$$

Substitute this expression for $y$ into equation (2):

$$
3x - 2(3x - 1) + z = -1
$$

$$
3x - 6x + 2 + z = -1
$$

$$
-3x + z + 2 = -1 \implies z = 3x - 3
$$

Substitute both $y = 3x - 1$ and $z = 3x - 3$ into equation (1):

$$
2x + (3x - 1) - 2(3x - 3) = 3
$$

$$
2x + 3x - 1 - 6x + 6 = 3
$$

$$
-x + 5 = 3 \implies x = 2
$$

Now calculate the values of $y$ and $z$:

$$
y = 3(2) - 1 = 5
$$

$$
z = 3(2) - 3 = 3
$$

So the vector $\mathbf{F}$ is:

$$
\mathbf{F} = 2\mathbf{a}_1 + 5\mathbf{a}_2 + 3\mathbf{a}_3
$$

## Type-04: Position Vectors and Resultant from Coordinates

### Solved Problem 20

#### Question

(a) Find the position vectors $\mathbf{r}_1$ and $\mathbf{r}_2$ for the points $P(2,4,3)$ and $Q(1,-5,2)$ in terms of the unit vectors $\mathbf{i}$, $\mathbf{j}$, $\mathbf{k}$.

(b) Determine analytically the resultant of these position vectors.

#### Solution

**Part (a)**

The position vector of any point $A(x,y,z)$ starts at the origin $O(0,0,0)$ and ends at $A$. It is defined by the following equation:

$$
\mathbf{r} = x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j} + z\mathbf{k}
$$

For point $P(2,4,3)$, the position vector is:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 = 2\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}
$$

For point $Q(1,-5,2)$, the position vector is:

$$
\mathbf{r}_2 = \mathbf{i} - 5\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}
$$

**Part (b)**

The resultant vector is the sum of the two position vectors:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 + \mathbf{r}_2 = (2\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}) + (\mathbf{i} - 5\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k})
$$

To find the sum, we add the corresponding components:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 + \mathbf{r}_2 = (2+1)\mathbf{i} + (4-5)\mathbf{j} + (3+2)\mathbf{k}
$$

This gives the final resultant vector:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 + \mathbf{r}_2 = 3\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k}
$$

## Type-05: Vector Magnitude Formula Proof

### Solved Problem 21

#### Question

Prove that the magnitude $A$ of the vector $\mathbf{A} = A_1\mathbf{i} + A_2\mathbf{j} + A_3\mathbf{k}$ is:

$$
A = \sqrt{A_1^2 + A_2^2 + A_3^2}
$$

#### Solution

We represent the vector $\mathbf{A}$ as the diagonal of a rectangular box. One corner of the box is at the origin $O(0,0,0)$. The opposite corner is at $P(A_1, A_2, A_3)$.

The projection of point $P$ on the $xy$-plane is $Q(A_1, A_2, 0)$.

The projection of point $Q$ on the $x$-axis is $R(A_1, 0, 0)$.

In the $xy$-plane, triangle $ORQ$ is a right-angled triangle. The right angle is at $R$.

By the Pythagorean theorem:

$$
OQ^2 = OR^2 + RQ^2
$$

We know that $OR = A_1$ and $RQ = A_2$. This gives:

$$
OQ^2 = A_1^2 + A_2^2
$$

Now look at triangle $OQP$ in space. The line segment $QP$ is perpendicular to the $xy$-plane. So the angle at $Q$ is a right angle.

By the Pythagorean theorem:

$$
OP^2 = OQ^2 + QP^2
$$

We know that $QP = A_3$. We substitute the value of $OQ^2$:

$$
OP^2 = A_1^2 + A_2^2 + A_3^2
$$

The magnitude $A$ is the length of $OP$. We take the square root of both sides:

$$
A = \sqrt{A_1^2 + A_2^2 + A_3^2}
$$

This completes the proof.
