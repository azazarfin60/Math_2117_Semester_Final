## Type-03: Cross Product Operations, Perpendicular Vectors, and Areas

### Solved Problem 16

#### Question

Determine a unit vector perpendicular to the plane of $\mathbf{A} = 2\mathbf{i} - 6\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}$ and $\mathbf{B} = 4\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}$.

#### Solution

Let vector $\mathbf{C} = c_1\mathbf{i} + c_2\mathbf{j} + c_3\mathbf{k}$ be perpendicular to the plane of $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$. 

So $\mathbf{C}$ is perpendicular to both $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$. Their dot products are zero:

$$
\mathbf{C} \cdot \mathbf{A} = 2c_1 - 6c_2 - 3c_3 = 0 \implies 2c_1 - 6c_2 = 3c_3
$$

$$
\mathbf{C} \cdot \mathbf{B} = 4c_1 + 3c_2 - c_3 = 0 \implies 4c_1 + 3c_2 = c_3
$$

We solve this linear system. Multiply the second equation by 2:

$$
8c_1 + 6c_2 = 2c_3
$$

Add this equation to the first equation:

$$
10c_1 = 5c_3 \implies c_1 = \frac{1}{2}c_3
$$

Substitute $c_1$ into the second equation:

$$
4\left( \frac{1}{2}c_3 \right) + 3c_2 = c_3
$$

$$
2c_3 + 3c_2 = c_3 \implies 3c_2 = -c_3 \implies c_2 = -\frac{1}{3}c_3
$$

Write out vector $\mathbf{C}$ using $c_3$:

$$
\mathbf{C} = c_3\left( \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{i} - \frac{1}{3}\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k} \right)
$$

Now calculate the magnitude of $\mathbf{C}$:

$$
C = |c_3|\sqrt{\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2 + \left(-\frac{1}{3}\right)^2 + 1^2}
$$

$$
= |c_3|\sqrt{\frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{9} + 1} = |c_3|\sqrt{\frac{49}{36}} = \frac{7}{6}|c_3|
$$

The unit vector is:

$$
\mathbf{u} = \pm \frac{\mathbf{C}}{C} = \pm \frac{\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{i} - \frac{1}{3}\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}}{7/6}
$$

$$
= \pm \left( \frac{3}{7}\mathbf{i} - \frac{2}{7}\mathbf{j} + \frac{6}{7}\mathbf{k} \right)
$$

### Solved Problem 21

#### Question

Prove $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = -\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{A}$.

#### Solution

Let $\mathbf{C} = \mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}$. Its magnitude is:

$$
C = AB \sin \theta
$$

The direction of $\mathbf{C}$ is determined by the right-hand rule. The vectors $\mathbf{A}$, $\mathbf{B}$, and $\mathbf{C}$ form a right-handed system.

Now let $\mathbf{D} = \mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{A}$. Its magnitude is:

$$
D = BA \sin \theta
$$

Since $AB = BA$, the magnitude of $\mathbf{D}$ is equal to the magnitude of $\mathbf{C}$. 

The direction of $\mathbf{D}$ is determined by the right-hand rule for $\mathbf{B}$ and $\mathbf{A}$. Pointing fingers from $\mathbf{B}$ to $\mathbf{A}$ gives a direction opposite to $\mathbf{C}$.

So vector $\mathbf{D}$ has the same magnitude but the opposite direction:

$$
\mathbf{C} = -\mathbf{D} \implies \mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = -\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{A}
$$

The proof is complete.

### Solved Problem 22

#### Question

If $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = \mathbf{0}$ and if $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ are not zero, show that $\mathbf{A}$ is parallel to $\mathbf{B}$.

#### Solution

We write out the cross product definition:

$$
\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = AB \sin \theta \mathbf{u}
$$

We are given that $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = \mathbf{0}$:

$$
AB \sin \theta \mathbf{u} = \mathbf{0}
$$

Since vectors $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ are non-zero, their magnitudes $A$ and $B$ are not zero. So we have:

$$
\sin \theta = 0
$$

This equation has two solutions for the angle:

$$
\theta = 0^\circ \quad \text{or} \quad \theta = 180^\circ
$$

In both cases, the vectors lie along the same line. So they are parallel.

### Solved Problem 28

#### Question

If $\mathbf{A} = 2\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}$ and $\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}$, find:
(a) $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}$
(b) $\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{A}$
(c) $(\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B}) \times (\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{B})$

#### Solution

**Part (a)**

Use the determinant formula:

$$
\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
2 & -3 & -1 \\
1 & 4 & -2
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}((-3)(-2) - (-1)(4)) - \mathbf{j}((2)(-2) - (-1)(1)) + \mathbf{k}((2)(4) - (-3)(1))
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(6 + 4) - \mathbf{j}(-4 + 1) + \mathbf{k}(8 + 3)
$$

$$
= 10\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} + 11\mathbf{k}
$$

**Part (b)**

Using the anti-commutative property:

$$
\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{A} = -\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}
$$

$$
= -10\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} - 11\mathbf{k}
$$

**Part (c)**

First, calculate the sum and difference vectors:

$$
\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B} = (2\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}) + (\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}) = 3\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{B} = (2\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}) - (\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}) = \mathbf{i} - 7\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}
$$

Evaluate the cross product:

$$
(\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B}) \times (\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{B}) = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
3 & 1 & -3 \\
1 & -7 & 1
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}((1)(1) - (-3)(-7)) - \mathbf{j}((3)(1) - (-3)(1)) + \mathbf{k}((3)(-7) - (1)(1))
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(1 - 21) - \mathbf{j}(3 + 3) + \mathbf{k}(-21 - 1)
$$

$$
= -20\mathbf{i} - 6\mathbf{j} - 22\mathbf{k}
$$

*Alternative Method:*

$$
(\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{B}) \times (\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{B}) = \mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{A} - \mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} + \mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{A} - \mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{B}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{0} - \mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} + \mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{A} - \mathbf{0}
$$

$$
= -2(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B})
$$

Using the result from Part (a):

$$
= -2(10\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} + 11\mathbf{k}) = -20\mathbf{i} - 6\mathbf{j} - 22\mathbf{k}
$$

### Solved Problem 29

#### Question

If $\mathbf{A} = 3\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}$, $\mathbf{B} = 2\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}$, and $\mathbf{C} = \mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}$, find:
(a) $(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \times \mathbf{C}$
(b) $\mathbf{A} \times (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C})$

#### Solution

**Part (a)**

First, calculate the cross product inside the parentheses:

$$
\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
3 & -1 & 2 \\
2 & 1 & -1
\end{vmatrix} = -\mathbf{i} + 7\mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k}
$$

Now calculate the outer cross product:

$$
(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \times \mathbf{C} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
-1 & 7 & 5 \\
1 & -2 & 2
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}((7)(2) - (5)(-2)) - \mathbf{j}((-1)(2) - (5)(1)) + \mathbf{k}((-1)(-2) - (7)(1))
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(14 + 10) - \mathbf{j}(-2 - 5) + \mathbf{k}(2 - 7)
$$

$$
= 24\mathbf{i} + 7\mathbf{j} - 5\mathbf{k}
$$

**Part (b)**

First, calculate the cross product inside the parentheses:

$$
\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
2 & 1 & -1 \\
1 & -2 & 2
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}((1)(2) - (-1)(-2)) - \mathbf{j}((2)(2) - (-1)(1)) + \mathbf{k}((2)(-2) - (1)(1))
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(2 - 2) - \mathbf{j}(4 + 1) + \mathbf{k}(-4 - 1)
$$

$$
= 0\mathbf{i} - 5\mathbf{j} - 5\mathbf{k}
$$

Now calculate the outer cross product:

$$
\mathbf{A} \times (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
3 & -1 & 2 \\
0 & -5 & -5
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}((-1)(-5) - (2)(-5)) - \mathbf{j}((3)(-5) - (2)(0)) + \mathbf{k}((3)(-5) - (-1)(0))
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(5 + 10) - \mathbf{j}(-15) + \mathbf{k}(-15)
$$

$$
= 15\mathbf{i} + 15\mathbf{j} - 15\mathbf{k}
$$

*Note:* The result in Part (a) is different from Part (b). This shows that the vector cross product is not associative.

### Solved Problem 30

#### Question

Prove that the area of a parallelogram with sides $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ is $|\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}|$.

#### Solution

Let $h$ be the height of the parallelogram. If $\theta$ is the angle between vectors $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$, then:

$$
h = A \sin \theta
$$

The area of the parallelogram is the product of its base and its height:

$$
\text{Area} = B h = B (A \sin \theta) = AB \sin \theta
$$

By definition, the magnitude of the cross product is:

$$
|\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}| = AB \sin \theta
$$

So the area is equal to $|\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}|$.

### Solved Problem 32

#### Question

Determine a unit vector perpendicular to the plane of $\mathbf{A} = 2\mathbf{i} - 6\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}$ and $\mathbf{B} = 4\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}$.

#### Solution

The cross product vector $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}$ is perpendicular to both vectors. So it is perpendicular to their plane:

$$
\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
2 & -6 & -3 \\
4 & 3 & -1
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(6 - (-9)) - \mathbf{j}(-2 - (-12)) + \mathbf{k}(6 - (-24))
$$

$$
= 15\mathbf{i} - 10\mathbf{j} + 30\mathbf{k}
$$

Calculate the magnitude of this cross product:

$$
|\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}| = \sqrt{15^2 + (-10)^2 + 30^2}
$$

$$
= \sqrt{225 + 100 + 900} = \sqrt{1225} = 35
$$

A unit vector perpendicular to the plane is:

$$
\mathbf{u} = \pm \frac{\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}}{|\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}|}
$$

$$
= \pm \frac{15\mathbf{i} - 10\mathbf{j} + 30\mathbf{k}}{35}
$$

$$
= \pm \left( \frac{3}{7}\mathbf{i} - \frac{2}{7}\mathbf{j} + \frac{6}{7}\mathbf{k} \right)
$$

### Supplementary Problem 65

#### Question

If $\mathbf{A} = 4\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}$ and $\mathbf{B} = -2\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}$, find a unit vector perpendicular to both $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$.

#### Solution

The cross product is perpendicular to both vectors:

$$
\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\

$$

$$
= -\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}
$$

Calculate the magnitude of the cross product:

$$
|\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}| = \sqrt{(-1)^2 + 2^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt{1 + 4 + 4} = 3
$$

A unit vector perpendicular to both is:

$$
\mathbf{u} = \pm \frac{-\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}}{3}
$$

$$
= \pm \left( \frac{\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}}{3} \right)
$$

### Supplementary Problem 78

#### Question

Evaluate each of the following:
(a) $2\mathbf{j} \times (3\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{k})$
(b) $(\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j}) \times \mathbf{k}$
(c) $(2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{k}) \times (\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j})$
(d) $(4\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}) \times (3\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{k})$
(e) $(2\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}) \times (3\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} + 4\mathbf{k})$

#### Solution

**Part (a)**

$$
2\mathbf{j} \times (3\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{k}) = 6(\mathbf{j} \times \mathbf{i}) - 8(\mathbf{j} \times \mathbf{k})
$$

$$
= 6(-\mathbf{k}) - 8(\mathbf{i}) = -8\mathbf{i} - 6\mathbf{k}
$$

**Part (b)**

$$
(\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j}) \times \mathbf{k} = (\mathbf{i} \times \mathbf{k}) + 2(\mathbf{j} \times \mathbf{k})
$$

$$
= (-\mathbf{j}) + 2(\mathbf{i}) = 2\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j}
$$

**Part (c)**

$$
(2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{k}) \times (\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j}) = 2(\mathbf{i} \times \mathbf{i}) + 4(\mathbf{i} \times \mathbf{j}) - 4(\mathbf{k} \times \mathbf{i}) - 8(\mathbf{k} \times \mathbf{j})
$$

$$
= 2(\mathbf{0}) + 4(\mathbf{k}) - 4(\mathbf{j}) - 8(-\mathbf{i})
$$

$$
= 8\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j} + 4\mathbf{k}
$$

**Part (d)**

$$
(4\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}) \times (3\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{k}) = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
4 & 1 & -2 \\
3 & 0 & 1
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}((1)(1) - (-2)(0)) - \mathbf{j}((4)(1) - (-2)(3)) + \mathbf{k}((4)(0) - (1)(3))
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(1) - \mathbf{j}(4 + 6) + \mathbf{k}(-3)
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i} - 10\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}
$$

**Part (e)**

$$
(2\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}) \times (3\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} + 4\mathbf{k}) = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
2 & 1 & -1 \\
3 & -2 & 4
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}((1)(4) - (-1)(-2)) - \mathbf{j}((2)(4) - (-1)(3)) + \mathbf{k}((2)(-2) - (1)(3))
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(4 - 2) - \mathbf{j}(8 + 3) + \mathbf{k}(-4 - 3)
$$

$$
= 2\mathbf{i} - 11\mathbf{j} - 7\mathbf{k}
$$

### Supplementary Problem 80

#### Question

If $\mathbf{A} = \mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}$, $\mathbf{B} = 2\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}$ and $\mathbf{C} = \mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}$, find:
(a) $|(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \times \mathbf{C}|$
(b) $|\mathbf{A} \times (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C})|$
(c) $\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C})$
(d) $(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \cdot \mathbf{C}$
(e) $(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \times (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C})$
(f) $(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B})(\mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{C})$

#### Solution

**Part (a)**

First, calculate the cross product inside the parentheses:

$$
\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
1 & -2 & -3 \\
2 & 1 & -1
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(2 - (-3)) - \mathbf{j}(-1 - (-6)) + \mathbf{k}(1 - (-4))
$$

$$
= 5\mathbf{i} - 5\mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k}
$$

Now calculate the outer cross product:

$$
(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \times \mathbf{C} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
5 & -5 & 5 \\
1 & 3 & -2
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(10 - 15) - \mathbf{j}(-10 - 5) + \mathbf{k}(15 - (-5))
$$

$$
= -5\mathbf{i} + 15\mathbf{j} + 20\mathbf{k}
$$

Find the magnitude:

$$
|(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \times \mathbf{C}| = \sqrt{(-5)^2 + 15^2 + 20^2}
$$

$$
= \sqrt{25 + 225 + 400} = \sqrt{650} = 5\sqrt{26}
$$

**Part (b)**

First, calculate the cross product inside the parentheses:

$$
\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
2 & 1 & -1 \\
1 & 3 & -2
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(-2 - (-3)) - \mathbf{j}(-4 - (-1)) + \mathbf{k}(6 - 1)
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k}
$$

Now calculate the outer cross product:

$$
\mathbf{A} \times (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
1 & -2 & -3 \\
1 & 3 & 5
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(-10 - (-9)) - \mathbf{j}(5 - (-3)) + \mathbf{k}(3 - (-2))
$$

$$
= -\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k}
$$

Find the magnitude:

$$
|\mathbf{A} \times (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C})| = \sqrt{(-1)^2 + (-8)^2 + 5^2}
$$

$$
= \sqrt{1 + 64 + 25} = \sqrt{90} = 3\sqrt{10}
$$

**Part (c)**

Using the result of $\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}$ from Part (b):

$$
\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = (\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}) \cdot (\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k})
$$

$$
= (1)(1) + (-2)(3) + (-3)(5)
$$

$$
= 1 - 6 - 15 = -20
$$

**Part (d)**

Using the result of $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}$ from Part (a):

$$
(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \cdot \mathbf{C} = (5\mathbf{i} - 5\mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k}) \cdot (\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k})
$$

$$
= (5)(1) + (-5)(3) + (5)(-2)
$$

$$
= 5 - 15 - 10 = -20
$$

**Part (e)**

We use the vector triple product identity:

$$
\mathbf{X} \times (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = \mathbf{B}(\mathbf{X} \cdot \mathbf{C}) - \mathbf{C}(\mathbf{X} \cdot \mathbf{B})
$$

Let $\mathbf{X} = \mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}$:

$$
(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \times (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = \mathbf{B}((\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \cdot \mathbf{C}) - \mathbf{C}((\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \cdot \mathbf{B})
$$

Since the cross product $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}$ is perpendicular to $\mathbf{B}$:

$$
(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}) \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0
$$

Use the scalar value from Part (d):

$$
= \mathbf{B}(-20) - \mathbf{C}(0) = -20\mathbf{B}
$$

$$
= -20(2\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k})
$$

$$
= -40\mathbf{i} - 20\mathbf{j} + 20\mathbf{k}
$$

**Part (f)**

Calculate the dot product:

$$
\mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{C} = (2\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}) \cdot (\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k})
$$

$$
= (2)(1) + (1)(3) + (-1)(-2)
$$

$$
= 2 + 3 + 2 = 7
$$

Multiply the vector $\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B}$ by this scalar:

$$
(\mathbf{A} \times \mathbf{B})(\mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{C}) = 7(5\mathbf{i} - 5\mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k})
$$

$$
= 35\mathbf{i} - 35\mathbf{j} + 35\mathbf{k}
$$

### Supplementary Problem 83

#### Question

Find the area of a triangle with vertices at $(3,-1,2)$, $(1,-1,-3)$ and $(4,-3,1)$.

#### Solution

Let the three vertices of the triangle be:

$$
P(3,-1,2), \quad Q(1,-1,-3), \quad R(4,-3,1)
$$

Define two vectors representing the sides:

$$
\mathbf{PQ} = (1 - 3)\mathbf{i} + (-1 - (-1))\mathbf{j} + (-3 - 2)\mathbf{k} = -2\mathbf{i} - 5\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{PR} = (4 - 3)\mathbf{i} + (-3 - (-1))\mathbf{j} + (1 - 2)\mathbf{k} = \mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}
$$

The area of the triangle is:

$$
\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} |\mathbf{PQ} \times \mathbf{PR}|
$$

Find the cross product:

$$
\mathbf{PQ} \times \mathbf{PR} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
-2 & 0 & -5 \\
1 & -2 & -1
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(0 - 10) - \mathbf{j}(2 - (-5)) + \mathbf{k}(4 - 0)
$$

$$
= -10\mathbf{i} - 7\mathbf{j} + 4\mathbf{k}
$$

Calculate the magnitude:

$$
|\mathbf{PQ} \times \mathbf{PR}| = \sqrt{(-10)^2 + (-7)^2 + 4^2}
$$

$$
= \sqrt{100 + 49 + 16} = \sqrt{165}
$$

Calculate the area:

$$
\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{165}
$$

## Type-04: Vector Applications in Mechanics

### Solved Problem 17

#### Question

Find the work done in moving an object along a vector $\mathbf{r} = 3\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} - 5\mathbf{k}$ if the applied force is $\mathbf{F} = 2\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}$.

#### Solution

The work done is defined as the dot product of the force and the displacement:

$$
W = \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{r}
$$

Substitute the vectors:

$$
W = (2\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}) \cdot (3\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} - 5\mathbf{k})
$$

$$
= (2)(3) + (-1)(2) + (-1)(-5)
$$

$$
= 6 - 2 + 5 = 9
$$

The work done is 9.

## Type-05: Equations of Planes and Distances

### Solved Problem 18

#### Question

Find an equation for the plane perpendicular to the vector $\mathbf{A} = 2\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} + 6\mathbf{k}$ and passing through the terminal point of the vector $\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}$.

#### Solution

Let $Q(1,5,3)$ be the terminal point of $\mathbf{B}$. Let $P(x,y,z)$ be any point on the plane with position vector $\mathbf{r} = x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j} + z\mathbf{k}$.

The vector $\mathbf{QP}$ lies entirely in the plane:

$$
\mathbf{QP} = \mathbf{r} - \mathbf{B}
$$

Since vector $\mathbf{A}$ is perpendicular to the plane, $\mathbf{QP}$ is perpendicular to $\mathbf{A}$:

$$
(\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{B}) \cdot \mathbf{A} = 0 \implies \mathbf{r} \cdot \mathbf{A} = \mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{A}
$$

Substitute the coordinate vectors:

$$
(x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j} + z\mathbf{k}) \cdot (2\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} + 6\mathbf{k}) = (\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}) \cdot (2\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} + 6\mathbf{k})
$$

Calculate the dot products:

$$
2x + 3y + 6z = (1)(2) + (5)(3) + (3)(6)
$$

$$
= 2 + 15 + 18 = 35
$$

The equation of the plane is:

$$
2x + 3y + 6z = 35
$$

### Solved Problem 19

#### Question

In Solved Problem 18, find the distance from the origin to the plane.

#### Solution

The distance $d$ from the origin to the plane is the length of the projection of vector $\mathbf{B}$ onto the normal vector $\mathbf{A}$.

First, find the unit vector $\mathbf{a}$ in the direction of $\mathbf{A}$:

$$
\mathbf{a} = \frac{\mathbf{A}}{A}
$$

Calculate the magnitude of $\mathbf{A}$:

$$
A = \sqrt{2^2 + 3^2 + 6^2} = \sqrt{4 + 9 + 36} = 7
$$

Write out the unit vector:

$$
\mathbf{a} = \frac{2}{7}\mathbf{i} + \frac{3}{7}\mathbf{j} + \frac{6}{7}\mathbf{k}
$$

Calculate the distance using the dot product:

$$
d = \mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{a} = (\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}) \cdot \left( \frac{2}{7}\mathbf{i} + \frac{3}{7}\mathbf{j} + \frac{6}{7}\mathbf{k} \right)
$$

$$
= (1)\left(\frac{2}{7}\right) + (5)\left(\frac{3}{7}\right) + (3)\left(\frac{6}{7}\right)
$$

$$
= \frac{2}{7} + \frac{15}{7} + \frac{18}{7} = \frac{35}{7} = 5
$$

The distance from the origin to the plane is 5.

### Solved Problem 45

#### Question

Find an equation for the plane determined by the points $P_1(2, -1, 1)$, $P_2(3, 2, -1)$ and $P_3(-1, 3, 2)$.

#### Solution

Let the position vectors of the three points be:

$$
\mathbf{r}_1 = 2\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{r}_2 = 3\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{r}_3 = -\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}
$$

Let $P(x,y,z)$ be a general point on the plane with position vector $\mathbf{r} = x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j} + z\mathbf{k}$.

Define three vectors lying in the plane:

$$
\mathbf{P_1P} = \mathbf{r} - \mathbf{r}_1 = (x - 2)\mathbf{i} + (y + 1)\mathbf{j} + (z - 1)\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{P_1P_2} = \mathbf{r}_2 - \mathbf{r}_1 = \mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} - 2\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
\mathbf{P_1P_3} = \mathbf{r}_3 - \mathbf{r}_1 = -3\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}
$$

Since these vectors all lie in the same plane, they are coplanar. Their scalar triple product is zero:

$$
\mathbf{P_1P} \cdot (\mathbf{P_1P_2} \times \mathbf{P_1P_3}) = 0
$$

First, calculate the cross product:

$$
\mathbf{P_1P_2} \times \mathbf{P_1P_3} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
1 & 3 & -2 \\
-3 & 4 & 1
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= \mathbf{i}(3 - (-8)) - \mathbf{j}(1 - 6) + \mathbf{k}(4 - (-9))
$$

$$
= 11\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j} + 13\mathbf{k}
$$

Now calculate the dot product:

$$
((x - 2)\mathbf{i} + (y + 1)\mathbf{j} + (z - 1)\mathbf{k}) \cdot (11\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j} + 13\mathbf{k}) = 0
$$

$$
11(x - 2) + 5(y + 1) + 13(z - 1) = 0
$$

$$
11x - 22 + 5y + 5 + 13z - 13 = 0
$$

$$
11x + 5y + 13z = 30
$$

So the equation of the plane is:

$$
11x + 5y + 13z = 30
$$

### Supplementary Problem 77

#### Question

Given that $\mathbf{A} = 3\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}$ and $\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} - 4\mathbf{k}$ are the position vectors of points $P$ and $Q$ respectively:
(a) Find an equation for the plane passing through $Q$ and perpendicular to line $PQ$.
(b) What is the distance from the point $(-1,1,1)$ to the plane?

#### Solution

**Part (a)**

The line $PQ$ has the direction of vector $\mathbf{PQ}$:

$$
\mathbf{PQ} = \mathbf{A} - \mathbf{B} = (3 - 1)\mathbf{i} + (1 - (-2))\mathbf{j} + (2 - (-4))\mathbf{k}
$$

$$
= 2\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} + 6\mathbf{k}
$$

The plane is perpendicular to vector $\mathbf{PQ}$ and passes through $Q(1,-2,-4)$.

Let $R(x,y,z)$ be a general point on the plane with position vector $\mathbf{r} = x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j} + z\mathbf{k}$. 

The vector $\mathbf{QR} = \mathbf{r} - \mathbf{B}$ lies in the plane, so it is perpendicular to the normal vector $\mathbf{PQ}$:

$$
(\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{B}) \cdot \mathbf{PQ} = 0 \implies \mathbf{r} \cdot \mathbf{PQ} = \mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{PQ}
$$

Substitute the components:

$$
(x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j} + z\mathbf{k}) \cdot (2\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} + 6\mathbf{k}) = (\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j} - 4\mathbf{k}) \cdot (2\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j} + 6\mathbf{k})
$$

$$
2x + 3y + 6z = (1)(2) + (-2)(3) + (-4)(6)
$$

$$
2x + 3y + 6z = 2 - 6 - 24 = -28
$$

So the equation of the plane is:

$$
2x + 3y + 6z = -28
$$

**Part (b)**

The distance $d$ from point $(x_0, y_0, z_0) = (-1,1,1)$ to plane $2x + 3y + 6z + 28 = 0$ is:

$$
d = \frac{|Ax_0 + By_0 + Cz_0 + D|}{\sqrt{A^2 + B^2 + C^2}}
$$

$$
d = \frac{|2(-1) + 3(1) + 6(1) + 28|}{\sqrt{2^2 + 3^2 + 6^2}}
$$

$$
d = \frac{|-2 + 3 + 6 + 28|}{\sqrt{4 + 9 + 36}} = \frac{35}{7} = 5
$$

The distance is 5.

### Supplementary Problem 90

#### Question

Find the volume of the parallelepiped whose edges are represented by $\mathbf{A} = 2\mathbf{i} - 3\mathbf{j} + 4\mathbf{k}$, $\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} - \mathbf{k}$, $\mathbf{C} = 3\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} + 2\mathbf{k}$.

#### Solution

The volume of a parallelepiped is the absolute value of the scalar triple product:

$$
V = |\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C})|
$$

By Solved Problem 38, we calculate this product using a determinant:

$$
\mathbf{A} \cdot (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{C}) = \begin{vmatrix}
2 & -3 & 4 \\
1 & 2 & -1 \\
3 & -1 & 2
\end{vmatrix}
$$

$$
= 2((2)(2) - (-1)(-1)) - (-3)((1)(2) - (-1)(3)) + 4((1)(-1) - (2)(3))
$$

$$
= 2(4 - 1) + 3(2 + 3) + 4(-1 - 6)
$$

$$
= 2(3) + 3(5) + 4(-7) = 6 + 15 - 28 = -7
$$

Take the absolute value to get the volume:

$$
V = |-7| = 7
$$

The volume is 7.

### Supplementary Problem 92

#### Question

Find the constant $a$ such that the vectors $2\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} + \mathbf{k}$, $\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}$ and $3\mathbf{i} + a\mathbf{j} + 5\mathbf{k}$ are coplanar.

#### Solution

Three vectors are coplanar if their scalar triple product is zero:

$$
\begin{vmatrix}
2 & -1 & 1 \\
1 & 2 & -3 \\
3 & a & 5
\end{vmatrix} = 0
$$

Expand the determinant by the first row:

$$
2((2)(5) - (-3)(a)) - (-1)((1)(5) - (-3)(3)) + 1((1)(a) - (2)(3)) = 0
$$

$$
2(10 + 3a) + 1(5 + 9) + 1(a - 6) = 0
$$

$$
20 + 6a + 14 + a - 6 = 0
$$

Combine the terms:

$$
7a + 28 = 0 \implies 7a = -28 \implies a = -4
$$

So the constant is $a = -4$.
