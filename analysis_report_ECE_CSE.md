## 10. Strategic Preparation Guide

### 10.1 🔴 Tier 1 — Guaranteed Topics (prepare with 100% confidence)

1. **Rank of a matrix** — reduce to echelon/canonical form
2. **System of linear equations** — consistency check, solve with row reduction
3. **Eigenvalues & eigenvectors** — 3×3 matrices
4. **Basis & dimension** — of subspaces in $\mathbb{R}^n$ and polynomial spaces
5. **Green's theorem** — statement, proof, and verification
6. **Cross/dot product** — unit vectors, area of triangle, projections

### 10.2 🟡 Tier 2 — High Probability (appeared 5–7 times)

7. **Directional derivative** — especially $P=4e^{2x-y+z}$ at $(1,1,-1)$
8. **Line integrals** — parametric curve evaluation
9. **Volume integrals** — bounded regions
10. **Matrix inverse** — via adjoint or row operations
11. **Symmetric + skew-symmetric decomposition** proof
12. **∇ identity proofs** — $\nabla\times(\nabla\times\vec{A})$, $\nabla^2(1/r)=0$

### 10.3 🟢 Tier 3 — Moderate Probability (appeared 3–4 times)

13. **Divergence theorem** verification
14. **Stokes' theorem** — statement + proof
15. **Cayley-Hamilton theorem** — verify + find $A^{-1}$
16. **ODE by matrix method** — $dx/dt=ax+by$ systems
17. **Linear independence** proofs
18. **$(AB)^{-1}=B^{-1}A^{-1}$** proof
19. **Curvature & torsion** computation

### 10.4 🔵 Tier 4 — CSE-Only / Rare Topics

20. **Kernel & image** of linear mappings
21. **Linear operator matrix representation** in non-standard bases
22. **Hilbert space** — definition and properties
23. **Frenet-Serret formulae**
24. **Direct sum** definition and examples
25. **Commutator maps**

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **For ECE students:** Focus on Tiers 1–3. Mastering these 19 topics covers **95%+** of historical exam content.
>
> **For CSE students:** Prepare Tiers 1–4. The extra abstract topics in Tier 4 carry **15–25 marks** per paper and are what differentiate CSE exams from ECE.

## 13. Conclusion & Final Recommendations

1. **Cross-department study is highly valuable** — ECE and CSE share ~70% of their question pool, making papers from either department excellent practice material for the other.

2. **The top 5 topics alone cover ~50% of total marks** across all papers: Rank, Systems of Equations, Eigenvalues, Basis/Dimension, and Green's Theorem.

3. **Question recycling is real and increasing** — expect 25–38% of questions to be variants of past papers. Solving all 10 papers thoroughly is the single best preparation strategy.

4. **CSE students should supplement with ECE papers** for additional practice on shared computational topics, while also preparing the CSE-exclusive theoretical topics (Hilbert space, kernel/image, Frenet-Serret).

5. **The 72→60 mark transition** means modern papers require faster execution with less time per mark — practice under timed conditions.
