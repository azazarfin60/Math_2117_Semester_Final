## Page 111

$$\cos \theta = \frac{\langle e\_1 , e\_2 \rangle}{\|e\_1\| \|e\_2\|} = \frac{0}{1 \cdot 1} = 0$$

The angle between $e\_1$ and $e\_2$ is $90^\circ$.
Similarly, the angle between $e\_1$ and $e\_3$ and $e\_3$ and $e\_2$ is $90^\circ$.

So, $\{ (1,0,0) , (0,1,0) , (0,0,1) \}$ form an orthogonal basis of $\mathbb{R}^3$.

### Orthogonal basis of R^n
$$\{ \underbrace{(1,0,0 \dots 0)}_{n \text{ terms}} , (0,1,0 \dots 0) , \dots , (0,0 \dots 1) \}$$

### Distance of $u$ & $v$:-
$$\left( \|u-v\| \right)$$

$$u = (1,2,3)$$
$$v = (4,5,6)$$
$$d(u,v) = \sqrt{(1-4)^2 + (2-5)^2 + (3-6)^2} = \sqrt{27}$$

$\star$ **Orthogonal হলেই তারা একে অপরের linearly independent.**
