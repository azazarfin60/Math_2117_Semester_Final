# B.Sc. Engineering 2nd Year Odd Semester Examination, 2023
**Course No: Math 2113**
**Course Title: Vector Analysis and Linear Algebra**
**Time: 03 Hours**
**Full Marks: 60**

---

### SECTION - A

**1(a)** Determine whether the vectors 

$$
\vec{A} = \vec{i} - 3\vec{j} + 2\vec{k}, \quad \vec{B} = 2\vec{i} - 4\vec{j} - \vec{k}, \quad \vec{C} = 3\vec{i} + 2\vec{j} - \vec{k}
$$

are linearly dependent or independent. **(03)**

**1(b)** Prove that a necessary and sufficient condition for the vectors $\vec{A}$, $\vec{B}$ and $\vec{C}$ to be coplanar is that: **(03)**

$$
\vec{A} \cdot (\vec{B} \times \vec{C}) = 0
$$

**1(c)** Show that the acceleration $\vec{a}$ of a particle which travels along a space curve with velocity $\vec{v}$, is given by: **(04)**

$$
\vec{a} = \frac{dv}{dt} \vec{T} + \frac{v^2}{\rho} \vec{N}
$$

where $\vec{T}$ is the unit tangent vector to the space curve, $\vec{N}$ is its unit principal normal and $\rho$ is the radius of curvature.

**2(a)** For the space curve $\vec{r} = \vec{r}(t)$, prove that: **(05)**
*   (i) Curvature 

    $$
    k = \frac{|\dot{\vec{r}} \times \ddot{\vec{r}}|}{|\dot{\vec{r}}|^3}
    $$

*   (ii) Torsion 

    $$
    \tau = \frac{[\dot{\vec{r}} \quad \ddot{\vec{r}} \quad \dddot{\vec{r}}]}{|\dot{\vec{r}} \times \ddot{\vec{r}}|^2}
    $$

**2(b)** Find the directional derivative of $P = 4e^{2x-y+z}$ at the point $(1, 1, -1)$ in a direction towards the point $(-3, 5, 6)$. **(03)**

**2(c)** If $\vec{A}$ and $\vec{B}$ are irrotational, prove that $\vec{A} \times \vec{B}$ is solenoidal. **(02)**

**3(a)** If 

$$
\vec{F} = 2xy\vec{i} + 4y^2\vec{j}
$$

, evaluate: **(05)**

$$
\int_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r}
$$

acting along the piecewise smooth curve consisting of line segments from $(-2, 2)$ to $(0,0)$ then to $(2,3)$.

**3(b)** If 

$$
\vec{F} = 2xz\vec{i} - x\vec{j} + y^2\vec{k}
$$

, evaluate: **(05)**

$$
\iiint_V \vec{F} dV
$$

where $V$ is the region bounded by the surfaces $x = 0$, $y = 0$, $y = 6$, $z = x^2$, and $z = 4$.

**4(a)** Verify divergence theorem for 

$$
\vec{A} = (x^2 - z^2)\vec{i} + 2xy\vec{j} + (y^2 + z)\vec{k}
$$

taken over the region bounded by the cylinder $y^2 + z^2 = 4$ and the planes $x = 0$, $x = 2$. **(05)**

**4(b)** State the Stoke's theorem. Discuss Stoke's theorem geometrically. Also write down the difference between Stoke's theorem and Green's theorem. **(05)**

### SECTION - B

**5(a)** Prove that: **(03)**

$$
(AB)^{-1} = B^{-1} A^{-1}
$$

provided that $A$ and $B$ are non-singular matrices.

**5(b)** If $A$ is an involutory matrix then show that $\frac{1}{2}(I + A)$ and $\frac{1}{2}(I - A)$ are idempotent matrices and: **(03)**

$$
\frac{1}{2}(I + A) \cdot \frac{1}{2}(I - A) = 0
$$

where $I$ is an identity matrix.

**5(c)** Define the rank of a matrix. Reduce the following matrix: **(04)**

$$
A = \begin{pmatrix}
3 & -4 & -1 & 2 \\
1 & 7 & 3 & 3 \\
5 & -2 & 5 & 4 \\
9 & -3 & 7 & 7
\end{pmatrix}
$$

into normal form and hence find its rank.

**6(a)** Test whether the following system of equations is consistent or not. If consistent then find the complete solution: **(05)**

$$
\begin{aligned}
3x - 2y - t &= 2 \\
x - 2y - 3z + 2t &= 3 \\
2y + 2z + t &= 1 \\
y + 2z + t &= 1
\end{aligned}
$$

**6(b)** Find the inverse of the following matrix: **(05)**

$$
A = \begin{pmatrix}
2 & 4 & 3 & 2 \\
3 & 6 & 5 & 2 \\
2 & 5 & 2 & -3 \\
4 & 5 & 14 & 14
\end{pmatrix}
$$

**7(a)** Find the eigen values and corresponding eigen vectors of the following matrix: **(05)**

$$
A = \begin{pmatrix}
2 & 1 & 1 \\
1 & 2 & 1 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{pmatrix}
$$

**7(b)** Using the matrix method, solve the following linear system: **(05)**

$$
\frac{dx}{dt} = 7x - y, \quad \frac{dy}{dt} = 4x + 3y
$$

with initial conditions $x(0) = 1$ and $y(0) = 3$.

**8(a)** Write the vector $v = (2, -5, 3)$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$ as a linear combination of the vectors: **(03)**

$$
e_1 = (1, -3, 2), \quad e_2 = (2, -4, -1), \quad e_3 = (1, -5, 7)
$$

**8(b)** Determine whether or not the following form a basis for the vector space $\mathbb{R}^3$: **(04)**
*   (i) $(1, 1, 1)$ and $(1, -1, 5)$
*   (ii) $(1, 2, 3)$, $(1, 0, -1)$, $(3, -1, 0)$ and $(2, 1, -2)$
*   (iii) $(1, 1, 1)$, $(1, 2, 3)$ and $(2, -1, 1)$
*   (iv) $(1, 1, 2)$, $(1, 2, 5)$ and $(5, 3, 4)$

**8(c)** Let $T: \mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}^3$ be the linear mapping defined by:

$$
T(x, y, z) = (x + 2y - z, y + z, x + y - 2z)
$$

Find a basis and the dimension of the kernel $W$ of $T$. **(03)**
